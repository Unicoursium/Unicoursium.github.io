Task 1 – Sinusoidal Signal Generation

Created a Matlab function sine_gen.m to generate a sinewave with given amplitude, frequency, sampling rate, and duration.

Generate a sine wave

Observation

• The generated waveform is periodic and smooth.

• Increasing frequency results in more oscillations over the same time window.

• Sampling frequency controls time resolution of the plotted signal.

Learning

• Matlab functions can be used to encapsulate signal generation.

• Discrete-time signals are represented as sampled versions of continuous signals.

Task 2 – Spectrum of a Signal

Implemented plot_spec.m using Matlab’s FFT function to plot the frequency spectrum of a sinewave.

Observation

• A pure 400 Hz sinewave produces a clear peak at 400 Hz in the frequency domain.

• The spectrum is symmetric around zero frequency.

• Higher sampling frequency increases frequency resolution.

Learning

• FFT converts a signal from time domain to frequency domain.

• Pure tones correspond to single frequency components.

Task 3 – Two-Tone Signal

Generated two sinewaves:

• s1: 400 Hz, amplitude 1.0

• s2: 1000 Hz, amplitude 0.5

and added them together.

Observation

• Time-domain waveform becomes more complex due to superposition.

• Frequency spectrum shows two distinct peaks at 400 Hz and 1000 Hz.

Learning

• Linear systems allow signals to be added directly.

• Frequency components remain separable in the spectrum.

Task 4 – Two Tones with Noise

Added Gaussian noise using randn(size(sig)) to the two-tone signal.

Observation

• Time-domain signal becomes irregular.

• Frequency spectrum still shows the main frequency peaks, but with a raised noise floor.

Learning

• Noise affects signal clarity but dominant frequencies can still be identified.

• Frequency-domain analysis is more robust to noise than time-domain inspection.

Task 5 – Projection Using Dot Product

Computed dot products between different sinusoidal signals.

Results

• Dot product of s1 (400 Hz) and s2 (1000 Hz) = 1.8789e-13

• Dot product of s1 (400 Hz) and s3 (401 Hz) =-1.9702e-10

• Dot product of (s1 + s2) with s1 = 5000

Learning

• Sinusoids of different frequencies are orthogonal over a full period.

• Dot product measures similarity between signals.

• Projection shows how much of one signal exists within another.

Task 6 – PyBench Board as Spectrum Analyser

Used the PyBench board to display the FFT of microphone input on the OLED screen.

Observation

• The spectrum updates in real time when a pure tone is played.

• Peaks correspond to the frequency of the input sound.

• Display range is 0–5 kHz with magnitude shown in dB.

Learning

• FFT can be implemented on embedded hardware in real time.

• Frequency-domain analysis is useful for real-world signal inspection.
