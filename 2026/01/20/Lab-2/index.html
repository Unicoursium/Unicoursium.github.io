Task 1: Connecting Pybench to PC

Check the connection

In task 1, we have connected our Pybench to the PC, and we’ve seen the disk in our File explorer.

Self-Test

Procedure

  • Set the DIP switch to 110

  • Pressed the RESET button on the PyBench board

  • Observed the OLED display

  • Pressed the USR button to cycle through self-test modes

Observation

  • OLED displayed “PyBench Self-Test” after reset

  • First press of USR button:

    • Real-time microphone waveform displayed

    • Signal amplitude changed in response to ambient sound

  • Second press:

    • Accelerometer test showing pitch and roll values when tilting the board

Conclusion

  • Microphone, ADC, OLED display, and IMU are operating correctly

  • PyBench hardware passed the built-in self-test

Task 2: Signal Generation

Sine Wave

  • Time domain: smooth periodic waveform

  • Frequency domain: single dominant peak at the fundamental frequency

  • Minimal harmonic content

Triangle Wave

  • Time domain: linear rising and falling edges

  • Frequency domain: odd harmonics present

  • Harmonic amplitudes decrease rapidly with frequency

Square Wave

  • Time domain: sharp transitions

  • Frequency domain: strong odd harmonics

  • Harmonics decay more slowly than triangle wave
