Task 4: Windowing Effect on Signal Spectrum

Method

  • Replaced plot_spec with plot_spec_dB to display spectrum in dB

  • Captured tuning fork signals at 1000 Hz and 1100 Hz

  • Applied Hamming window before FFT

  • Compared spectra with and without windowing

Observations

Without Window (Rectangular Window)

  • Significant spectral leakage around the main peak

  • Energy spreads into adjacent frequency bins

  • Nearby frequencies are difficult to distinguish

With Hamming Window

  • Reduced spectral leakage

  • Main peak becomes cleaner and more localized

  • Adjacent frequency components are easier to separate

Key Findings

  • Truncating a signal is equivalent to applying a rectangular window

  • Windowing reduces spectral leakage at the cost of peak widening

  • Hamming window improves frequency discrimination in practice
